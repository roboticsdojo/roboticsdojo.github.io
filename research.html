⁠Development of an Open-Source ROS Interface for the KUKA KR6 R900-2 Industrial Robot Arm

KUKA industrial robots are reliable and power moder industries, but are limited by their closed-source nature and the inflexible KUKA Robot Language (KRL), which lacks advanced programming, library extensions, and affordable integration with external devices.

To overcome this, we present the KUKA KVP Command Interface, an open-source ROS-based interface for the KR6 R900-2.

It enables reading/writing KUKA variables via KUKAVARPROXY, supporting advanced algorithms, custom motion planning, filtering, and seamless integration of diverse input/output devices within the familiar and extensible ROS ecosystem for research and development.

⁠In-Situ Monitoring and Control of Robotic Wire Arc Additive Manufacturing for On-Demand Part Production

This research project develops a robust Wire Arc Additive Manufacturing (WAAM) system with in-situ monitoring and closed-loop control for producing metal parts.

A KUKA KR6 R900-2 robot arm integrated with a MIG welding torch will be equipped with sensors to continuously measure key process variables during deposition.

Real-time data processing will be used to identify deviations from desired build conditions, enabling automatic adjustments to wire feed rate, travel speed, and arc parameters.

This approach will improve part quality, repeatability, and efficiency.
